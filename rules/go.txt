GO (SIMPLIFIED)

An ancient strategy game of territorial control, played on a 9x9 board.

HOW TO PLAY
- Black goes first. Players alternate placing stones on empty intersections.
- Click an intersection to place your stone.
- Surround opponent stones to capture them (remove all their liberties).
- Pass your turn if you have no useful moves. Two consecutive passes end the game.

RULES
- A liberty is an empty point directly adjacent to a stone or connected group.
- A group with zero liberties is captured and removed from the board.
- Ko Rule: You may not immediately recapture a single stone that just captured yours.
- Self-capture (suicide) is not allowed.
- Scoring: Territory (empty points you surround) + Captured stones.
- The player with the higher score wins.
